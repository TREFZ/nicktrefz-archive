Video Production

At TopCoder I suggested, demonstrated, and executed various video projects to help demonstrate the company's technological nature. Velcro elimination brackets became elaborate video displays, printed brochures became commercials, and a guy with a microphone and a sheet of paper became a prerecorded and produced audio and video demonstration.

You'll find several examples of my contributions below.

This video shows what we called "competitor introductions". Whereas we used to just read their names over the loudspeaker, I turned the event into a show. The display in the background is a reusable Flash app that I built from scratch that gathers data from XML feeds. The audio is a mix of my own voice, soundbytes from the press, and some music samples.
Tools: Flash, Audacity

This is an animation that I produced as part of a series of short video clips that we looped at our onsite events on giant projection displays. A set of brackets has always been the recognizable symbol for TopCoder, so I combined that with the concept of evolution from my own slogan for TopCoder, "Competition Evolved"
Tools: Carrara 3D basic 2, Flash

Here is another example of a short video we looped at our onsite events. This one was based on the phrase "TopCoder. All over the world." meant to convey our global presence, in a sci-fi theme.
Tools: Carrara 3D Basic 2, Flash

Here is another example of a short video we looped at our onsite events. This one simply presents the event logo in a stylish manner.
Tools: Carrara 3D Basic 2

We held our biannual events at various cities and facilities over the years, which meant we needed to constantly update our set design to work with each location. I created 3D mockups like this to help the team picture the room layout.
Tools: Carrara 3D Basic 2.

This is a fun little movie that I created at our last event, the TCO08. I propped up a webcam in the corner of the room and set it to snap a picture every N seconds over the six days that we were there. I then spliced them together into a movie that shows the entire six days in just six minutes.
Tools: Yawcam, VirtualDub
